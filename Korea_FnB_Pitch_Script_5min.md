# Korea F&B Sell-through Intelligence

### 5-minute client pitch script · English

**Audience** · global hedge fund · long-only · sell-side analyst · VC
**Goal** · get them to ask for the sample pack
**Tone** · confident, specific, not salesy · let the numbers do the work
**Length** · ~720 words · ~5 minutes at a measured pace

> Stage notes in italics. Pauses marked with [...]. Numbers and brand names should land hard · slow down on them.

---

## [0:00–0:30] · The hook

Korean food brands are revaluing right now.

Samyang Foods · the maker of Buldak ramen · is up nearly four-fold in two years. CJ CheilJedang's Bibigo line is now a billion-dollar brand abroad. Orion's Choco Pie is a category leader in three countries outside Korea.

K-food is no longer a domestic story. It is a global category that listed Korean equities are pricing in real time. [...]

To trade it, you need to see the demand. And in Korea, you can't.

*[Pause. Let that sit.]*

---

## [0:30–1:15] · The problem · Korea is an alt-data desert

In the US, you have Nielsen, NPD, Numerator, Circana. Standard kit.

In Korea? Nothing equivalent.

The channels are fragmented across CU, GS25, E-Mart, Coupang, Lotte. There is no syndicated retail measurement. Earnings prints land cold · with four to six weeks of demand already past. Sell-side models are guesses. Discretionary funds size positions blind into the print.

We have been talking to Korean consumer-data owners for two years to close that gap. Today we have something nobody else does.

---

## [1:15–2:45] · The solution · Two partners, one demand signal

We pair the two largest consumer-data assets in Korea, exclusively.

**First · CU.** The number-one convenience-store chain · roughly 31% share of Korea's convenience market. Daily, SKU-level POS sell-through.

The convenience channel is where new ramen flavors, RTD coffee, cigarettes, soju launches show up first. CU sees them on day one.

**Second · Lotte Members.** Forty million-plus members across Lotte Mart, Lotte Department, Lotte Duty-Free, Lohb's, and 7-Eleven Korea. That's the same shopper, observed across hypermarket, department, and tourist channels.

CU reads the trend first. Lotte confirms it broadly across the rest of retail. [...]

Neither is complete alone. Together · they cover the moments where Korean consumer brands actually monetize.

Daily. T+1. Down to the SKU.

---

## [2:45–4:00] · Two products · Breadth and Depth

We deliver in two resolutions.

**Product One · Breadth.**

The top twenty listed Korean F&B names by market cap. Each with their five flagship brands. One hundred brand rows. Brand-by-day grain.

You see Buldak versus Shin Ramyun versus Saero soju in one panel. Across ramen, snacks, dairy, beverage, alcohol, HMR, and tobacco. This is your pre-earnings screen for the whole listed universe.

**Product Two · Depth.**

The two most investable Korean F&B categories · ramen and alcohol · taken down to the actual SKU. Three hundred and thirty-four SKUs across twenty-two brands and eight companies. Pack format. Volume. Promotion flags. Price points.

This is where you see the Buldak Carbo cup outpacing the original cup. Where you catch Saero gaining share at Chum Churum's expense before it shows up in HiteJinro's segment print.

Same underlying tape. Two resolutions. [...] You pick the one that fits the trade.

---

## [4:00–4:45] · What it buys you · Concrete examples

Three quick examples.

**One.** Buldak export momentum was visible in CU POS roughly four weeks before Samyang Foods guided up. That's a sized long, not a guess.

**Two.** When CJ CheilJedang guides Bibigo growth on the call, you can confirm in the Lotte hypermarket panel before the close. Or fade if the data disagrees. We have seen both happen.

**Three.** Tobacco. KT&G's mix between combustible and next-gen products · the lil line · is the entire investment thesis. You see that mix daily in CU.

Plus everything else · the Saero versus Chamisul share war. Pepero Day cliff in November. Imported-beer rotation. Pack-format shifts inside Hoegaarden. All real-time, all tied to a listed ticker plus our taxonomy code.
